The geosphere is the solid part of the Earth, including the rocks, minerals, soil, and landforms. The geosphere is constantly changing due to processes such as erosion, weathering, plate tectonics, volcanism, and earthquakes. The geosphere provides the foundation for life on Earth, as well as resources such as metals, fossil fuels, and water.

The biosphere is the part of the Earth where life exists, including all living organisms and their interactions. The biosphere covers most of the surface of the Earth, from the deepest oceans to the highest mountains. The biosphere is diverse and complex, with millions of species of plants, animals, fungi, bacteria, and other microorganisms. The biosphere is also dynamic and adaptive, as living things evolve, reproduce, and respond to environmental changes.

.3

The atmosphere is the layer of gases that surrounds the Earth, extending from the surface to the outer space. The atmosphere is composed of mainly nitrogen, oxygen, argon, and carbon dioxide, as well as water vapor and other trace gases. The atmosphere protects the Earth from harmful radiation, regulates the climate, and enables weather phenomena such as clouds, rain, snow, and wind. The atmosphere also supports life by providing oxygen for respiration, carbon dioxide for photosynthesis, and nitrogen for nitrogen fixation.
